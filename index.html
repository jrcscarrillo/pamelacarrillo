About Me

Hola! I am Pamela Carrillo and currently working on my Ph.D. dissertation project at the Surface Chemical Dynamics and Catalysis research group at Brookhaven National Laboratory run by senior chemist Michael G. White, Ph.D. My research project is the fruit of my love for chemistry and nature: the development of a way to enable ethanol production from biomass-derived synthetic gas (CO/CO2/H2).

My scientific endeavor started when I earned my bachelor’s in Chemistry from Pontifical Catholic University of Ecuador located in Quito, Ecuador, where I am originally from. During my undergrad years, I earned two academic excellence awards, was part of the Exact and Natural Sciences Department students organization, and of the Computational Chemistry Group at my university. These and many other accomplishments made me recipient of a Fulbright Scholarship and a Delta Kappa Gamma International Fellowship to pursue my master’s studies in the U.S. in 2013. As a graduate student, I have been selected as participant of the São Paulo School of Advanced Sciences on Recent Developments in Synchrotron Radiation in Brazil in summer 2015 and the 2016 ACS Summer School on Green Chemistry & Sustainable Energy in Colorado, US.

The exposure to the latest scientific and technologic advances and my interest for surface chemistry has shaped my career interests. Thus, I aims to further pursue a career on the development of catalytic materials for sustainable chemical processes.

Resume
RESEARCH INTERESTS: Determination of catalysts’ structure under working conditions. Understanding the fundamentals of chemical conversions through heterogeneous and homogeneous catalysis. RESEARCH EXPERTISE: Characterization of powder catalysts using in-situ X-ray synchrotron and lab-based analytical techniques. Synthesis and catalytic testing of catalysts composed of nanoparticles supported on metal oxide powders. Establishment of relationships between the active state of catalysts under reaction conditions and their catalytic activity.
